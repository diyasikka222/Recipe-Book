VEG BIRYANI

Recipe Type : Veg

Ingredients Required :

  • 1.5 cups basmati rice
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 green cardamoms
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 large potato, diced
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • ½ cup green peas
  • ½ cup green beans, chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • ½ cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons chopped mint leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • A pinch of saffron threads soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)

Preparation Steps :

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in enough water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add the bay leaf, cardamoms, cloves, cinnamon stick, star anise, and salt. Add the soaked rice and cook until it's 70-80% done. Drain the rice and set aside.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies and sauté for a couple of minutes.
  4. Add the diced potatoes, carrots, cauliflower, green peas, and green beans. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Add the finely chopped tomatoes to the pan. Cook until they turn soft and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, biryani masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Lower the heat and add the yogurt. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes until the vegetables are cooked and coated with the masala.
  8. In a large pot or biryani handi, add a layer of the cooked vegetable masala. Add a layer of the partially cooked rice over the vegetable masala.
  9. Sprinkle chopped mint leaves, coriander leaves, and the saffron milk over the rice layer. Repeat the layers until all the rice and vegetable masala are used up. The topmost layer should be rice.
  10. Drizzle ghee over the topmost rice layer. You can also add some fried onions for garnish.
  11. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. You can seal the edges with dough to ensure no steam escapes. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes. Alternatively, you can place the pot in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for 20 minutes.
  12. Gently fluff the rice with a fork before serving. Serve hot with raita, salad, or any side dish of your choice.
Enjoy your homemade Veg Biryani!
