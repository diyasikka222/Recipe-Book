DAL MAKHNI

Recipe Type : Veg

Ingredients Required :

  • 1 cup whole black gram (sabut urad dal)
  • ¼ cup red kidney beans (rajma)
  • 4 cup water
  • salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 2 large tomatoes, finely chopped (or 1 cup tomato purée)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cream
  • 1 teaspoon dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander

Preparation Steps :

  1. Rinse the whole black gram and red kidney beans under running water. Soak them together in enough water for at least 8 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked dal and rajma. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked dal, rajma, 4 cups of water, salt, and turmeric powder. Cook on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes (or until they are soft and cooked through). Once cooked, mash them lightly and set aside.
  3. In a large pan, heat the butter and oil together. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies to the pan and sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes (or tomato purée) to the pan. Cook until the tomatoes turn soft and the oil starts to separate from the mixture.
  6. Add red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well and cook the masala for another 5-7 minutes.
  7. Add the cooked dal and rajma mixture to the pan with the masala. Mix well to combine all the ingredients.
  8. Add a cup of water (or as needed to adjust the consistency). Simmer the dal on low heat for at least 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally. This helps to develop the flavors.
  9. In a separate small pan, heat the butter and add the dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi). Pour this tadka over the dal. Add the heavy cream and mix well.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  11. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
Enjoy your homemade Dal Makhni!
